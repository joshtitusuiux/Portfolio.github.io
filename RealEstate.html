Designing A Real Estate Agent's Website



Overview:
A real estate agent wanted a new website to increase his traffic and clientelle. We delivered the finished product to our client receiving a positive testimonial from our stakeholder.




Team:
5 UX Designers
Timeline:
January 11, 2024 - April 28, 2024.
My Role:
Lead UX Designer, UI Designer, Lead UX Researcher
Tools:
Power Points, Google Docs, Wix

















The Solution:
A new website for our client that received positive testimonials from both our client and research participants.











The Problem:
Design a website that increases business for a real estate agent while staying on brand with his pre-existing website.










Initial User Research:
I led a study over the course of 3 days to analyze the performance of the real estate website we had completed. I recruited five participants and asked them to “speak aloud” as they completed 3 simple tasks on the website through remote and in-person testing. I questioned them about their thought process in each session, and took notes while they completed the 3 tasks on the website.




1. Task 1: Explore the website and find a page that listed houses.
2. Task 2: Use the website search filter to specifically find housees with 3 bedrooms.
3. Task 3: Use the website search filter to specifically find housees with 3 bedrooms.
4. Task 4: Use the website to book a consultation session with the real estate agent.















Insights:
Our data signified that participants needed
more clarity when it came to the listing search
feature which we decided to swap out and add
more clearly defined labels. The study revealed
that the website was accessible, easy to use,
and user-friendly for almost all participants,
and that there were only still small areas
for improvement.
Reflection:
I am proud of the feedback and positive
testimonials we received from both our
client and from participants about the
product we created. My major regret with
this product that I learned from was focusing
so much on user research and UI design that
I didn't stop and consider gathering many
metrics to quantify how well our website was
improving, outside of testimonials. It would
have been a greater way to showcase the
success and beauty of the website. This is
something I have taken with me in my
Cross Roads Church digital redesign project.
